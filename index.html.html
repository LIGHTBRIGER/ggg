✦ Follow the Path ✦

The Rishikesh Journey

Every visitor walks the same ancient road. From the bridge to the cafe — a pilgrimage of the senses.

Stop 01

Lakshman Jhula

This iconic suspension bridge is the primary landmark to get you into the general vicinity. The cafe is located about 1.5 km away from the bridge. If you are coming from the main Rishikesh city center or railway station, you can take a shared auto-rickshaw (tuk-tuk) heading towards the Laxman Jhula taxi stand or the Tapovan main market.

🌉
1.5 km away
Stop 02

Patanjali International Yoga Foundation

Once you are in the Tapovan area on the main Badrinath Highway, this renowned yoga center acts as an excellent local marker. The cafe is situated just about 100 meters away from this foundation, meaning you are only a couple of minutes away on foot once you spot it.

🧘
100 meters away
Stop 03

The Beatles Café

Your final destination. Nestled right in the heart of Tapovan.

✦ Since 1968 ✦

Where It All Began

The Beatles came to Rishikesh seeking silence. They found songs instead. We've been playing them ever since.

George Arrives First

George Harrison and his wife Pattie arrive at Maharishi's ashram ahead of the others, drawn by a deep curiosity about Indian classical music and meditation.

1968
Feb 1968

The Whole Band Arrives

John, Paul, Ringo and their partners join George. For ten weeks, they meditate, write, and listen — to the river, the birds, the silence. Forty-eight songs emerge.

The White Album Takes Shape

"Blackbird," "Back in the USSR," "Dear Prudence" — songs that became anthems, written in a state of deep stillness by the Ganges. Rishikesh gave birth to legend.

1968
2001

The Café Opens Its Doors

Founded by those who grew up on Beatles records and Ganges mornings. A small wooden room, a few chairs, a guitar on the wall — and the right song always playing.

A Place on Every Traveller's Map

From backpackers to seekers, musicians to meditators — the café became a landmark. Not because of what we serve, but because of how it feels when you sit down.

Today
